# GSL Pedagogy Foundations

## 1. NEP 2020, in one page

The National Education Policy 2020 governs Indian education through 2040. The principles that shape GSL content:

**Conceptual clarity over rote learning.** Every lesson helps students understand *why* something is true, not just *that* it is. Always include the "why" before the "what".

**Experiential learning.** Content connects to what students can do, not just what they memorise. Hands-on activities, real-world problems, projects.

**Holistic development.** Cognitive, social, emotional, creative, physical. A lesson on fractions is also a lesson on problem-solving patience. Content should not be narrowly cognitive.

**Multilingualism.** Don't assume English is the student's first language. Hindi or regional language equivalents of technical terms in parentheses where helpful.

**Critical thinking and creativity.** Every topic has at least one "what would you do?" or "why might this be different?" question, not just "recall the definition".

**Inclusion and equity.** Examples, characters, scenarios reflect India's diversity. No default of male-protagonist-in-urban-setting. Rural, urban, all regions, all genders, all faiths (mentioned respectfully only when relevant).

**Indian roots.** Connect concepts to Indian thinkers, history, culture where authentic. Aryabhata for mathematics is authentic. Aryabhata for geography is forced.

**21st century skills.** Critical thinking, creativity, communication, collaboration. Many lessons include a brief moment where the student practises one of these.

## 2. NCF 2023, in one page

The National Curriculum Framework 2023 operationalises NEP 2020 for schools. Stage-wise structure:

**Foundational stage** (ages 3 to 8): play-based learning, oral language, early literacy and numeracy through stories, games, activities.

**Preparatory stage** (ages 8 to 11, Classes 3 to 5): transition to structured learning, reading and writing in mother tongue plus a second language, concept of subject areas.

**Middle stage** (ages 11 to 14, Classes 6 to 8): discipline-based learning, project work, introduction to inquiry.

**Secondary stage** (ages 14 to 18, Classes 9 to 12): multidisciplinary learning, specialisation begins but breadth maintained, higher-order thinking, exam readiness balanced with real-world readiness.

**Implications for content:**
- Foundational stage is mostly oral, visual, story-based. Minimal text.
- Preparatory can introduce text but leans on illustrations and activities.
- Middle stage can be text-heavy but must have clear visual breaks, examples, practice opportunities.
- Secondary can resemble adult educational material but with clear structure, varied assessment formats, and real-world connections.

**Competency focus.** NCF 2023 emphasises competencies over content coverage. A lesson on "the water cycle" is not about memorising evaporation-condensation-precipitation. It's about the competency "can explain how water moves through the environment and predict what happens if one part of the cycle is disrupted."

Every GSL lesson plan names the competencies it develops, not just the topics it covers.

## 3. ADDIE, instructional design framework

The framework GSL uses for building content systematically. Five phases:

### Analyse
Before writing anything, understand:
- Who the learner is (grade, background, prior knowledge)
- What the learning outcome is (specific, measurable)
- What the context is (programme, time available, resources)
- What the constraints are (exam alignment, partner requirements, language)

**Output:** a one-page brief. Every playbook's "Before you start" section is the Analyse phase.

### Design
Plan structure before producing content:
- Sequence of topics
- Pedagogical approach for each (direct instruction, inquiry, project, discussion)
- Assessment approach
- Time allocation
- Resources required

**Output:** an outline. For a lesson plan, slide-by-slide or section-by-section. For a course, week-by-week.

### Develop
Produce the actual content:
- Write the material
- Create visuals
- Build assessments
- Package together

**Output:** the deliverable (deck, document, assessment).

### Implement
Deliver to learners:
- Train teachers or trainers
- Provide materials
- Support delivery

**Output:** learners actually experiencing the content.

### Evaluate
Measure and iterate:
- Did learners hit the outcomes?
- What worked, what didn't?
- What changes for next time?

**Output:** a learning report and updates to the content.

Every GSL content piece travels all five phases. Content that skips Analyse usually fails. Content that skips Evaluate stagnates.

## 4. Bloom's taxonomy, grade-calibrated

The revised Bloom's taxonomy (2001), ladder of cognitive complexity:

1. **Remember** — recall facts and concepts
2. **Understand** — explain ideas
3. **Apply** — use information in new situations
4. **Analyse** — draw connections
5. **Evaluate** — justify a decision or stance
6. **Create** — produce new or original work

### Grade-appropriate distribution for assessments

**Classes 1 to 2** (age 6 to 8):
- Remember 60%, Understand 30%, Apply 10%
- Analyse / Evaluate / Create: minimal, through activities not formal questions

**Classes 3 to 5** (age 8 to 11):
- Remember 40%, Understand 35%, Apply 20%, Analyse 5%
- Evaluate / Create: through projects, not standard questions

**Classes 6 to 8** (age 11 to 14):
- Remember 25%, Understand 30%, Apply 25%, Analyse 15%, Evaluate 5%
- Create: through projects

**Classes 9 to 10** (age 14 to 16):
- Remember 15%, Understand 25%, Apply 30%, Analyse 20%, Evaluate 10%
- Create: projects and extended response

**Classes 11 to 12** (age 16 to 18):
- Remember 10%, Understand 20%, Apply 30%, Analyse 25%, Evaluate 15%
- Create: extensively through projects, essays, original work

These are guides, not iron rules. A Class 7 Social Studies assessment can skew higher on Analyse than Class 7 Mathematics. Use judgement, but stay within ±10% of these numbers.

### Verbs at each level (for writing questions)
- **Remember:** list, name, recall, identify, define
- **Understand:** explain, describe, summarise, compare, classify
- **Apply:** use, solve, demonstrate, illustrate, calculate
- **Analyse:** differentiate, organise, relate, deconstruct, attribute
- **Evaluate:** judge, critique, defend, justify, argue
- **Create:** design, construct, plan, produce, invent

### MCQ distractor rules
When writing multiple-choice questions, distractors (wrong options) must be:
- **Plausible** — a student who doesn't know the answer might pick them
- **Based on common misconceptions** — not random wrong answers
- **Similar in length and structure** to the correct answer (students pattern-match; don't give the answer away)
- **Not "all of the above" or "none of the above"** except in rare justified cases
- **Mutually exclusive** — only one is correct, no ambiguity

A distractor that's obviously wrong ("The Sun is made of cheese") is a wasted option.

## 5. Age-appropriateness rules

### Language
- **Classes 1 to 2:** One-syllable words dominate. Short sentences (under 8 words). No abstract nouns.
- **Classes 3 to 5:** Up to 3-syllable words. Sentences up to 12 words. Abstract nouns with concrete backup.
- **Classes 6 to 8:** Normal vocabulary, sentences up to 18 words, introduce subject-specific terms with definitions on first use.
- **Classes 9 to 10:** Adult-adjacent vocabulary, compound sentences, specialised terms expected.
- **Classes 11 to 12:** Full adult vocabulary, complex sentences, disciplinary terminology without handholding.

### Examples
- **Classes 1 to 2:** family, school, pets, food, play.
- **Classes 3 to 5:** community, local surroundings, familiar sports, basic technology.
- **Classes 6 to 8:** broader community, India's states, basic history, simple science experiments.
- **Classes 9 to 10:** national issues, global awareness at surface level, career hints, deeper science.
- **Classes 11 to 12:** national and global complexity, career specificity, research contexts.

### Visuals
- **Younger grades:** bright colours, rounded illustrations, friendly characters, lots of white space.
- **Older grades:** more sophisticated visuals, real-world photography acceptable, infographics, charts, less cartoonish.

### Cognitive load
- **Younger:** one concept per lesson, heavy repetition.
- **Middle:** two or three connected concepts per lesson.
- **Older:** multiple concepts per lesson with explicit connections.

## 6. CBSE conventions

Most GSL schools are CBSE-affiliated. Follow these conventions in every output:
- Spellings: British English (Indian standard).
- Numbers: written out below 10 (one, two, three); digits for 10 and above.
- Dates: DD-Month-YYYY (20 April 2026).
- Units: SI wherever possible.
- Currency: INR with ₹ or "Rs" prefix. Never $.
- Social Studies: follow CBSE's secular, pluralistic framing.
- Science: align with latest NCERT textbooks (check NCERT.nic.in for current edition).
- English literature: draw from NCERT (Honeysuckle, Honeycomb, Beehive, Flamingo, etc.) unless explicitly going beyond.
- Mathematics: notation follows NCERT conventions (e.g. "×" not "*" for multiplication in student-facing material).

## 7. Common GSL pedagogy mistakes to avoid

- **Lecture-heavy lessons.** If a 40-minute lesson plan has the teacher talking for 40 minutes, it's wrong. Maximum 15 minutes of direct instruction. Rest is activity, discussion, practice.
- **Assessments that only test Remember.** Even Class 3 assessments should have some Understand and Apply. A Class 10 assessment with 80% Remember fails NEP 2020.
- **Examples disconnected from student life.** A maths problem about stock prices for Class 6 is detached. Same problem framed as sharing mangoes at a family function works.
- **Gender defaults.** "Rohan solved the problem" as default for maths, "Priya baked the cake" for home-related. Vary deliberately. Girls solve maths problems; boys bake cakes; farmers are women; scientists are Muslim; no pattern, lots of realism.
- **Cultural defaults from AI tools.** AI tools trained largely on American content default to American examples (baseball not cricket, dollars not rupees, prom not Diwali). Catch and redirect every time.
- **Pedagogical shortcuts via "common sense".** "Just explain it clearly" is not pedagogy. Use the frameworks above.
